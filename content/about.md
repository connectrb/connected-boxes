**25 years of technology consulting. Thousands of whiteboards. Two daughters who still aren’t convinced I have a real job.**

According to my kids, I get paid to "talk and draw boxes."

They aren't wrong. After two and a half decades in the industry, I’ve realized that the most complex problems in the world are usually just a bunch of messy, disconnected boxes waiting for someone to draw the right lines between them.

I’ve spent my career helping organizations move from a scattered "What if?" to a clear "Here’s how." Here is what that looks like in practice:

    * **Bridging the Gap:** I translate "Executive Vision" into "Technical Reality" (and back again).
    * **Simplifying the Complex:** I spend a lot of time explaining strategy in plain English. If I can’t bring people along for the ride, I can’t build the solution.
    * **Building Connections:** Not just between systems, but between people. Technology is the easy part; human alignment is where the real work happens.

I’m currently sharing these stories and "box-drawing" lessons over at my blog, **Connected Boxes**.

At the end of the day, I’m just a dad trying to make technology approachable for humans—and having a bit of fun along the way.
